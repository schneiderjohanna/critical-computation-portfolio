Project #8 Experimental Camera

Description

This experimental camera detects and mirrors the current mood of its user. It can differentiate between three states of moods: happy, sad and sleepy - the states that I personally mostly find myself in currently. It detects the states based on the movement and position of the user's mouth and eyes.

Design Process

Initial Design Sketches

In the beginning of my design process I came up with three ideas. My ideas included creating an underwater scenario, creating a kaleidoscope as well as creating an 'emotional mirror'. For my Major Studio project, I am working with the expression of emotions via digital communication, which is why I decided to proceed with my latter idea. In order to visualize the respective mood I created icons that I would associate with each of them - flowers for happiness, teardrops for sadness and Zzz icons. Further, I was inspired for the visual design by a project by Petra Cortright, called 'VVEBCAM' (2007).

Reflection

While creating this camera I realized how difficult it is for a program like this to detect very slight emotional cues in the face of the user. Even though I tried my best to make it detect rather nuanced differences, the user has to show a very broad smile in order for the program to be able to detect happiness. If I were to develop this project further, I would like to improve on this aspect. Ideally I would like to make the mirror being able to detect an emotion that the user themselves is not even fully consciously aware of.
